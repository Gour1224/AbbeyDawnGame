Abbey Dawn: Last Stand

A Cooperative Campaign Game of Desperate Valor

"The shadows lengthen, but our resolve shines brighter. For Abbey Dawn, and for Sigmar!"
— Lord-Celestant Michael, Hammer of the Dawn

Introduction

A gleaming bastion of Order, long unyielding amidst the treacherous realms, now finds its very foundations assailed. The Kingdom of Abbey Dawn, comprising two distinct yet allied strongholds – the Silverwood Citadel, a realm of stern warriors and arcane scholars, and the Emerald Bastion, a verdant land of agile rangers and nature-aligned defenders – faces an existential threat from the encroaching forces of Chaos and Destruction. Players will control a band of valiant heroes from these kingdoms, working in desperate unity to complete vital quests, defend their hallowed lands, and push back the relentless tide of darkness that threatens to consume all. "Abbey Dawn: Last Stand" is a narrative-driven cooperative campaign, where every victory is hard-won and every defeat carries weight, shaping the destiny of a realm on the brink.

This game meticulously weaves the strategic positioning and dice-based combat of Age of Sigmar tabletop battles with the resource management and tactical card play of Age of Sigmar: Champions, forging an unparalleled cooperative experience.

Heroes of the Realms: Champions & Harbingers

In "Abbey Dawn: Last Stand," players command powerful heroes drawn from the Grand Alliances of Order, Chaos, Death, and Destruction. Each hero belongs to a specific unit type, granting them unique strengths, abilities, and a personal narrative that unfolds throughout the campaign.

Grand Alliance: Order

The forces of Order stand as a bulwark against the encroaching darkness, united in their purpose to defend the Mortal Realms.

  • Stormcast Eternals: Sigmar's chosen warriors, descending like lightning to strike down the foes of Order.
  • Aelf: Agile and cunning warriors, masters of both blade and arcane arts, defending their ancient groves and hidden realms.

Grand Alliance: Chaos

The insidious forces of Chaos seek to corrupt and dominate the realms, driven by the whims of their dark gods. This alliance encompasses the brutal **Daemons of Khorne**, the insidious **Daemons of Tzeentch**, and the zealous **Followers of Chaos**.

  • Daemons of Khorne: Brutal, axe-wielding daemons dedicated solely to the Blood God, seeking skulls and bloodshed above all else.
  • Daemons of Tzeentch: Devious daemons and mutating cultists of the Changer of Ways, twisting reality with arcane horrors.
  • Followers of Chaos: Mortal champions and cultists devoted to the Ruinous Powers, often leading the daemonic legions. If a unit card has no specific sub-faction unit type (e.g., DAEMON, AELF, GROT) associated with it, it is considered to be a Follower of Chaos unit and to be used with associated heroes.

Grand Alliance: Death

The chilling legions of Death seek to claim all living souls for their undying masters, bringing eternal silence to the realms.

  • Soulblight: Ancient, aristocratic vampires and their shambling legions of the undead, seeking to expand their dominion of undeath.
  • Spirit: Ethereal spirits bound by Nagash's will, a chilling host of vengeful ghosts whose touch brings despair.
  • Mordant: Twisted yet noble, these bestial warriors fight with primal ferocity, believing themselves to be valiant knights.

Grand Alliance: Destruction

These primal forces seek only to smash, consume, and lay waste to all in their path, leaving ruin in their wake.

  • Orruk: Massive, green-skinned brutes who live for nothing more than a good, brutal fight, delivering bone-shattering blows.
  • Grot: A chittering, cackling horde of grots, ravenous squigs, and lumbering troggoths, overwhelming foes with maddening numbers.

Units of the Realms: Loyal Companions & Hordes

Beyond the might of individual heroes, players can field supporting units to bolster their forces. These units are represented by Unit Cards. When a Unit Card is played, the card itself is deployed onto the battlefield near the summoning hero, acting as a distinct entity with its own simplified warscroll. These units offer additional tactical options, from holding objectives to providing flanking attacks, and their presence directly supplements the power and strategic options of the hero who brought them forth.

Grand Alliance: Order Units

  • Stormcast Eternals: Stalwart warriors like Liberators, forming the unbreakable shield of Sigmar.
  • Aelf: Agile archers and swift skirmishers, raining down precise volleys or outmaneuvering foes.

Grand Alliance: Chaos Units

  • Daemon: Brutal Bloodletters or gibbering Pink Horrors, direct manifestations of the Ruinous Powers.
  • Follower: Mortal cultists and warriors, forming the bulk of the Chaos incursions. If a unit card has no specific sub-faction unit type (e.g., DAEMON, AELF, GROT) associated with it, it is considered to be a Follower of Chaos unit and to be used with associated heroes.

Grand Alliance: Death Units

  • Soulblight: Armored Grave Guard or shambling Deadwalker Zombies, forming the legions of the undead.
  • Spirit: Ethereal Spirit Hosts, passing through obstacles to chill their foes.
  • Mordant: Ravenous Crypt Ghouls, swarming foes with their numbers.
  • Vampire: Elite blood-kin, often supplementing Soulblight forces.

Grand Alliance: Destruction Units

  • Orruk: Heavily armored 'Ardboyz, forming a tough core for any Waaagh!
  • Grot: Lunatic Squig Hoppers or hordes of Moonclan Grots, delivering chaotic charges or overwhelming numbers.

Generic Unit Types

Some unit types are not tied to a specific Grand Alliance and can be included in any hero's deck, provided they do not contradict the hero's primary unit type (e.g., an Aelf hero cannot have a Beast unit card). These represent broader categories of creatures or constructs found across the realms.

  • Ogor: Massive, brutish humanoids, often serving as shock troops or heavy hitters.
  • Vehicle: War machines or chariots, providing speed, ranged attacks, or devastating charges.
  • Beast: Wild creatures, monsters, or monstrous mounts, offering raw power and unpredictable ferocity.

Champion Units

A special type of Unit Card, Champion Units represent powerful, named individuals or elite formations that can be deployed by heroes. They function like regular Unit Cards but have additional strategic importance:

  • Glory Generation: While a Champion Unit is on the battlefield and highlighted (e.g., by being the active unit or by a hero's ability), its controlling player generates 1 additional Glory Token at the start of their Hero Phase.
  • Bonus Stats (Conceptual): Champion Unit cards feature small, colored circles on their bottom corners. A Red Circle indicates bonus Attacks (e.g., a '2' in a red circle adds 2 to its Attacks characteristic). A Green Circle indicates bonus Wounds (e.g., a '3' in a green circle adds 3 to its Wounds characteristic). These values are added to the unit's base profile when deployed.
  • Target for Abilities: Champion Units can be specifically targeted by friendly and enemy abilities, making them focal points of tactical play.
  • Triumphant Defeat (Enemy): If an enemy Champion Unit is destroyed, the player who dealt the final blow immediately gains 2 Glory Tokens, rewarding the elimination of a high-value target.

Gameboard & Layouts

The epic battles of Abbey Dawn: Last Stand unfold upon a battlefield measuring **50 inches by 40 inches**. This space allows for diverse tactical engagements, from sprawling open fields to claustrophobic ruins. An ideal game board layout for Abbey Dawn would feature a dynamic mix of terrain elements: central open areas for large engagements, dense forest sections (especially for Sylvaneth scenarios) providing cover and flanking routes, crumbling ruins offering choke points and elevated positions, and vital objectives strategically placed to encourage movement and conflict. Key features like rivers, hills, or blighted ground could further influence tactical decisions, creating a varied and challenging environment for every scenario.

Game Components (Conceptual)

  • Game Board/Tiles: Modular tiles representing Abbey Dawn's varied terrain, from the ancient forests to the crumbling citadel walls.
  • Hero Miniatures/Standees: Exquisitely detailed miniatures or standees representing all heroes listed in the "Heroes of the Realms" section.
  • Enemy Miniatures/Standees: A diverse array of miniatures or standees representing the various Chaos and Destruction units that plague Abbey Dawn.
  • Hero Decks (Champions TCG style): Each hero possesses a unique deck of 25-30 cards, comprising potent Ability, arcane Spell, divine Blessing, and deployable Unit cards, as well as Quest cards.
  • Glory Tokens: Gleaming tokens representing the vital resource used to fuel heroic actions and card plays.
  • Wound Tokens: Markers to track the grievous damage sustained by heroes and enemies alike.
  • Dice: A collection of D6s for resolving the ebb and flow of combat.
  • Scenario Cards/Book: A detailed compendium outlining the objectives, enemy setup, and unique rules for each mission within the campaign.
  • Quest Cards: Specific objective cards, drawn from a separate deck, that heroes can undertake to gain bonus Glory or other vital rewards.
  • Curse Deck: A deck of insidious cards drawn by enemies, unleashing negative effects upon heroes or twisting the very fabric of the battlefield.

Optimal Card Pool & Deck Sizes

To ensure balanced gameplay and strategic depth, the following guidelines are recommended for card pool and hero deck sizes:

  • Hero Deck Size: Each hero should have a starting deck of 25-30 cards. This allows for diverse strategies without overwhelming players and accommodates Unit Cards.
  • Card Type Distribution (per Hero Deck):
    • Ability Cards: 8-10 cards (utility, buffs, debuffs, movement, combat actions)
    • Spell Cards (for Wizards/Arcane heroes): 4-6 cards (magical effects, damage, control)
    • Blessing Cards: 1-2 cards (powerful, often single-use, game-changing effects)
    • Unit Cards: 3-5 cards (deployable units to supplement the hero)
    • Quest Cards: 2-3 cards (scenario-specific objectives or personal quests)
  • Draft Card Pool: The total pool of available cards for drafting (for upgrades or scenario-specific additions) should be approximately 50-75 unique cards per Grand Alliance, ensuring a wide variety of choices over a campaign. This pool is separate from the heroes' starting decks.

The Champions' Arsenal: Cards in Play

The heart of Abbey Dawn's defense lies not only in the heroes' might but in the strategic deployment of their unique card decks. These cards, drawing inspiration from the Age of Sigmar: Champions TCG, represent a hero's training, innate abilities, and the blessings they invoke. Each card has a Glory cost, representing the effort required to unleash its power.

Card Types Explained

Cards in Abbey Dawn: Last Stand function similarly to those in the Age of Sigmar: Champions CCG, providing a range of tactical options:

  • Ability Cards: Versatile cards that provide special effects, buffs to allies, debuffs to enemies, or unique actions, including combat actions. Cost: 1 Glory Token.
  • Spell Cards: These cards unleash potent magical effects and can be played by any hero. When a Spell card is played, it is placed on the battlefield. Its effects manifest based on the 'Manifestation Value' in its top-right corner when played in its starting upright position. The card may require rotation prior to the full activation of the spell's effects. Spell cards cost 1 Glory Token to cast but may require multiple hero phases to fully manifest unless an ability or rule says otherwise. Casting a Spell card does not trigger refreshing a spell rotation on another card.

    Manifesting Spells: When a Spell card is played, it is placed on the battlefield. At the start of each subsequent Player Phase, it rotates 45 degrees clockwise. The spell's effects manifest when the card has rotated enough that its Manifestation Value (in its top-right corner) is no longer visible (or reaches zero). Once its effects manifest, the card is then discarded. If a Spell card does not have a Manifestation Value, its effects manifest immediately upon play, and it is then discarded.

  • Blessing Cards: Rare and powerful, Blessing cards represent divine favor or extraordinary fortune. They usually provide one-time, game-changing effects, such as significant healing or a powerful, un-negatable attack. Cost: 3 Glory Tokens.
    • Blessing Limit: Players may have a maximum of 2 active Blessing cards on the battlefield per objective they currently control.
  • Unit Cards: These cards represent a group of supporting models that can be deployed to the battlefield. When played, the Unit Card itself is placed on the board near the summoning hero, acting as a distinct entity with its own simplified warscroll for movement, combat, and interaction with objectives. To perform actions with a deployed Unit Card (e.g., move, attack), the controlling hero must spend their own Glory Tokens (1 Glory per action unless specified). Cost: 1 Glory Token (unless it is a Champion Unit).
  • Champion Unit Cards: A special type of Unit Card representing a powerful individual or elite formation. Costs: 4 Glory Tokens.

    Bonus Stats (Conceptual): Champion Unit cards feature small, colored circles on their bottom corners. A Red Circle indicates bonus Attacks (e.g., a '2' in a red circle adds 2 to its Attacks characteristic). A Green Circle indicates bonus Wounds (e.g., a '3' in a green circle adds 3 to its Wounds characteristic). These values are added to the unit's base profile when deployed.

  • Activated Champion Units (rotated) generate 1 additional Glory Token at the start of their controlling hero's Hero Phase.

Active Cards on the Gameboard & Rotation

When a card (Unit, Quest, or a persistent Ability/Spell) is played and remains on the gameboard, it is considered 'active'.

  • At the start of each Player Phase, all active cards on the battlefield (except Champion Units) rotate 45 degrees clockwise.
  • **Exhaustion:** If an Ability, Spell, or Quest card rotates and the top-right corner of the card has no value, that card is considered exhausted and is discarded.
  • **Unit Card Persistence:** Unit cards (excluding Champion Units) continue to rotate and activate their inherent abilities accordingly, even if their top-right corner has no value. They are not discarded due to rotation unless explicitly stated by another card effect.
  • **Champion Units:** Champion Units do not automatically rotate. Instead, they rotate 45 degrees clockwise only when a specified card (e.g., an Ability or Spell card) is played by their controlling hero that matches a symbol in the top-right corner of the Champion Unit card.
  • **Activated Champion Units:** A Champion Unit that has been rotated from its starting position (i.e., is not in its initial, unrotated state) is considered 'activated'. Activated Champion Units generate 1 additional Glory Token at the start of their controlling hero's Hero Phase.

Core Game Mechanics

1. Game Turn Structure

Each round of "Abbey Dawn: Last Stand" is a desperate struggle, meticulously divided into two critical phases:

  1. Player Phase: All valiant heroes activate, their actions chosen by the players in an order determined by their collective strategy.
  2. Enemy Phase: The relentless hordes of darkness surge forth, their every move dictated by the cruel will of the game system.

Players must confer and decide the optimal order in which their heroes will activate during their phase, for timing can be the key to victory.

2. Player Phase

During a hero's activation, they perform the following steps in a precise sequence, unleashing their might upon the foes of Abbey Dawn:

Glory Generation

Each hero begins their activation with a unique amount of Starting Glory Tokens, as indicated on their warscroll. Additionally, heroes generate a fixed amount of Glory Tokens at the start of their activation, also indicated on their warscroll. The ebb and flow of battle can grant additional opportunities for Glory:

  • At the dawn of their activation, each hero generates their specified Glory per Turn.
  • Strategic Objectives: If a hero or a unit they control starts their activation holding an objective (i.e., they are the only units within 3" of an objective marker), they generate 1 additional Glory Token. This rewards tactical positioning and control of vital points.
  • Activated Champion Units: An 'activated' Champion Unit (one that has been rotated from its initial position) generates 1 additional Glory Token at the start of their controlling hero's Hero Phase.
  • Triumphant Defeat: When a hero or a unit they control defeats an enemy unit (including Champion Units) or champion, the controlling player immediately gains 1 additional Glory Token. If the defeated unit was an enemy Champion Unit, the player gains 2 Glory Tokens instead. This fuels further aggression and rewards decisive blows.

Heroic Prowess and Glory: The varying Starting Glory and Glory per Turn values on each hero's warscroll are designed to reflect their inherent power and strategic role. More powerful or agile heroes may have abilities that generate Glory in unique ways, or their cards may have lower Glory costs, allowing them to perform more actions or unleash more devastating effects in a single turn. Conversely, more resilient or slower heroes might have higher Glory costs for their powerful actions, emphasizing their deliberate, impactful actions.

Card Play & Actions

  • Heroes possess a hand of potent cards, drawn from their unique, character-specific deck.
  • Most cards (Ability, Unit, Spell) cost 1 Glory Token to play.
  • Quest cards cost 2 Glory Tokens to play.
  • Blessing cards cost 3 Glory Tokens to play.
  • Champion Unit cards cost 4 Glory Tokens to play.
  • Players may play any number of cards as long as they command sufficient Glory.
  • The playing of cards empowers heroes to perform crucial actions: swift movement, devastating attacks, or the invocation of unique abilities.
  • Glory Limit: A player may spend a maximum of 4 Glory Tokens during a single turn. This limit applies to all Glory spent on cards, unit activations, and card management actions.
Movement
  • Each hero possesses a base Movement characteristic. Playing specific Ability cards can modify this, allowing for heroic charges or cunning repositioning. Movement is measured from any point on the hero's base to any point on their base.
  • Example: Knight-Questor Kelly plays her 'Swift Charge' Ability card (cost 1 Glory), granting her additional movement to engage the foe.
Combat (via Ability Cards)
  • Combat is initiated by playing an Ability card (cost 1 Glory) that specifies an attack.
  • Each attack specifies its range (Melee or Ranged), number of targets, and combat profile (To Hit, To Wound, Rend, Damage).
  • To Hit: Roll D6s. Each roll equal to or greater than the target's Defence is a hit.
  • To Wound: For each hit, roll D6. Each roll equal to or greater than the target's Toughness is a wound.
  • Rend & Damage: Rend reduces the target's Save roll. Damage is inflicted for unsaved wounds.
  • Saves (via Ability Cards): When a unit suffers a wound, roll a D6. If the roll is equal to or greater than the unit's Save (after Rend), the wound is negated. Players can play Ability cards (cost 1 Glory) to modify Saves or negate damage.
  • Wounds: Unsaved wounds inflict Wounds. When a unit's Wounds taken equals or exceeds its maximum Wounds, it is removed from play.
Ability Cards
  • These versatile cards provide special effects, buffs, debuffs, or unique actions. Cost: 1 Glory Token.
  • Example: Spirit-Warden Vicki plays 'Strategic Reposition' (cost 1 Glory) to move herself and a nearby ally.
Spell Cards
  • These cards unleash potent magical effects and can be played by any hero. When a Spell card is played, it is placed on the battlefield. Its effects manifest based on the 'Manifestation Value' in its top-right corner when played in its starting upright position. The card may require rotation prior to the full activation of the spell's effects. Spell cards cost 1 Glory Token to cast but may require multiple hero phases to fully manifest unless an ability or rule says otherwise. Casting a Spell card does not trigger refreshing a spell rotation on another card.

    Manifesting Spells: When a Spell card is played, it is placed on the battlefield. At the start of each subsequent Player Phase, it rotates 45 degrees clockwise. The spell's effects manifest when the card has rotated enough that its Manifestation Value (in its top-right corner) is no longer visible (or reaches zero). Once its effects manifest, the card is then discarded. If a Spell card does not have a Manifestation Value, its effects manifest immediately upon play, and it is then discarded.

  • Example: A player plays 'Arcane Shield' (cost 1 Glory). This Spell card has a Manifestation Value of '1' in its top-right corner. It is placed on the battlefield. At the start of the next Player Phase, it rotates 45 degrees. Its Manifestation Value is now gone, so its effects manifest: all allied heroes within 6 inches of the casting hero gain a +1 bonus to their Save rolls. The card is then discarded.
Blessing Cards
  • Rare and powerful, Blessing cards provide one-time, game-changing effects. Cost: 3 Glory Tokens.
  • Example: Branchwraith Daniel plays 'Nature's Renewal' (cost 3 Glory) to heal all allied heroes within range.
Unit Cards
  • Represent supporting models deployed to the battlefield. Playing a Unit Card (cost 1 Glory, unless Champion Unit) places it on the board near the summoning hero.
  • To perform actions with a deployed Unit Card (move, attack), the controlling hero must spend their own Glory Tokens (1 Glory per action unless specified).
  • Example: A player plays a 'Liberator Unit' card (cost 1 Glory). Michael spends 1 Glory Token to move them and another 1 Glory Token for their attack.
Champion Unit Cards
  • Special Unit Cards representing powerful individuals. Cost: 4 Glory Tokens.
  • Activated Champion Units (rotated) generate 1 additional Glory Token at the start of their controlling hero's Hero Phase.
Quest Cards
  • Specific objectives played from hand. Placed on the board when played. Cost: 2 Glory Tokens.
  • When completed, a Quest card rotates 90 degrees clockwise. Once rotated 360 degrees, it is discarded. Certain abilities or spells may cause Quest cards to rotate.
  • Example: A 'Secure the Old Mill' Quest card requires a hero to end their activation within 3 inches of the objective for two consecutive turns. Upon completion, it rotates.
Reactive Card Play
  • Certain cards can be played outside your hero's normal activation in response to specific triggers. Cost: Normal Glory cost (1, 2, 3, or 4 Glory).
  • Example: An enemy attacks Kelly. Arion plays 'Azyrite Shield' (cost 1 Glory, Reactive) to grant Kelly a +1 Save bonus.
Glory for Card Management

Unspent Glory Tokens can be used to manipulate your hand and deck:

  • Draw Cards: Spend 1 Glory Token to draw 2 cards.
  • Shuffle Hand: Spend 2 Glory Tokens to shuffle your hand into your deck and draw new cards.
  • Replenish Glory: Discard 2 cards to gain 1 Glory Token (once per activation).

End of Activation

Once a hero concludes their actions, their activation ends. Players then choose the next hero to activate.

3. Enemy Phase

After all heroes and their deployed units have activated, enemies surge forth:

  1. Movement: Each enemy moves towards the nearest visible hero or deployed Unit Card. If none are visible, they advance towards the nearest objective.
  2. Attack: If an enemy is in range, it attacks. Enemies have a fixed combat profile. Targeted heroes/units roll Saves as normal.
  3. Curse Cards (Optional): In certain scenarios, players may draw a Curse card from the Curse deck, unleashing negative effects.

Keywords Index

The Age of Sigmar: Champions TCG, upon which this game's card mechanics are inspired, utilizes a variety of keywords that grant special rules or effects. Familiarize yourself with these vital terms:

  • Allegiance: The primary faction a hero belongs to (e.g., Stormcast Eternals, Khorne Bloodbound), determining card access and synergistic effects.
  • Arcane: Denotes a Spell card or an effect related to magical energies.
  • Glory: The primary resource generated by heroes each turn, used to pay for cards and activate abilities.
  • Blessing: A powerful, often one-time, beneficial effect, typically with a higher Glory cost.
  • Bulwark: Grants a bonus to Save rolls, representing strong defensive positioning or armour.
  • Champion Unit: A special type of Unit Card representing a powerful individual or elite formation, with unique Glory generation and defeat rewards.
  • Cleave: An Attack effect that allows a single attack to potentially hit multiple adjacent targets.
  • Curse: A negative effect applied to a hero or the battlefield, usually drawn from the Curse deck by enemies.
  • Damage: The amount of Wounds inflicted by a successful attack after Saves are resolved.
  • Defence: A hero's characteristic or a card effect that helps to negate incoming Wounds.
  • Divine: An effect or card that draws upon celestial or holy power, often associated with healing or protection.
  • Fury: An Attack effect that grants additional attacks or damage under specific conditions, often when charging or against weakened foes.
  • Guard: A Defence effect that allows a hero to protect an adjacent allied unit, taking the hit instead.
  • Heal: Restores Wounds to a unit.
  • Inspire: Provides a bonus to nearby allied units, often to their To Hit, To Wound, or Defence characteristics.
  • Melee: An attack or ability that can only target units in base contact or within a very short range (e.g., 1 inch).
  • Mortal Wound: A special type of Wound that cannot be saved by normal Save rolls, only by specific abilities or effects.
  • Pierce: An Attack effect that ignores a certain amount of the target's Save characteristic (similar to Rend, but a keyword for clarity).
  • Quest: A specific objective card that, when successfully completed, grants rewards or progresses the narrative.
  • Ranged: An attack or ability that can target units at a distance, specified by a range value (e.g., 12 inches).
  • Relocate: An ability that allows a unit to immediately move a short distance without counting as a full movement action.
  • Rend: Reduces the target's Save roll by a specified amount (e.g., Rend -1 means a 4+ Save becomes a 5+ Save).
  • Revive: An effect that returns a defeated unit to play, often with a limited number of Wounds.
  • Swift: Grants bonus movement or allows for movement outside of the normal movement phase.
  • Tactical: An ability that involves strategic positioning or manipulation of units on the battlefield.
  • Wound: A unit's health point. When a unit's Wounds taken equals or exceeds its maximum Wounds, it is defeated.

Warscrolls: Champions & Harbingers

Each hero and champion in Abbey Dawn: Last Stand is represented by a Warscroll, detailing their unique characteristics, weapons, and abilities, crucial for understanding their role on the battlefield. These are simplified for the game, focusing on core mechanics.

Lord-Celestant Michael

Campaign Progression: The Saga of Abbey Dawn

"Abbey Dawn: Last Stand" is not merely a series of battles, but a desperate saga played out as a series of linked scenarios, each advancing the desperate struggle against the encroaching darkness, pushing the heroes to their very limits. The campaign is structured into two main arcs: the initial defense and the climactic counter-offensive.

Introductory Scenarios: The Gathering Storm

These initial scenarios depict the first desperate struggles for Abbey Dawn, as the forces of Order react to the sudden and overwhelming invasion.

Scenario 1: The First Breach

Objective: The very walls of Abbey Dawn tremble as the initial Chaos incursions crash against them. Players must valiantly defend three key defensive positions along the outer walls for 5 harrowing turns, or bravely defeat the formidable Chaos Champion leading this initial, brutal assault.

Setup: The heroes begin their desperate stand within the hallowed confines of the walls. Units of Khorne and Tzeentch are strategically placed at designated breach points, poised to overwhelm the defenses.

"The air itself screams with their arrival, a cacophony of hatred and despair! Stand fast, brothers and sisters, for the fate of Abbey Dawn rests upon our blades!"
— Knight-Questor Kelly, The Unyielding Star

Scenario 2: The Whispering Woods

General Campaign Elements:

  • Scenario Objectives: Each scenario presents specific, vital objectives (e.g., defend a sacred location, eliminate a key enemy commander, escort a vulnerable VIP through perilous territory).
  • Persistent Wounds: The scars of battle endure. Wounds taken by heroes in one scenario might tragically carry over into the next, demanding strategic healing or forcing them to start with fewer Wounds, adding a layer of grim realism.
  • Upgrades: For every hard-won victory in a scenario or the completion of specific Quests, heroes might unlock new, more powerful cards for their decks or gain permanent, invaluable stat bonuses, reflecting their growth in the face of adversity.
  • Story Choices: Some pivotal scenarios might present players with difficult, branching choices that profoundly affect the campaign's narrative path, leading to different challenges and outcomes.

Drafting Your Force: Heroes & Units

Before embarking on a campaign or an adversarial battle, players will engage in a strategic Draft to assemble their force of Heroes and supporting Units. This ensures variety and replayability, as players must adapt their strategies based on available champions and their accompanying forces.

The game is optimized for 2-8 players. The number of heroes and units each player controls can be scaled:

  • 2 Players: Each player controls 3 heroes and drafts 3 Unit Cards to add to their heroes' decks.
  • 3-4 Players: Each player controls 2 heroes and drafts 2 Unit Cards to add to their heroes' decks.
  • 5-8 Players: Each player controls 1 hero and drafts 1 Unit Card to add to their hero's deck.
  1. Initial Hero Selection: For a cooperative campaign, each player first chooses one hero from the entire roster to be their primary champion. For adversarial mode, each player chooses one hero to be their faction leader.
  2. Hero Draft Rounds:
    • All remaining heroes not chosen in step 1 are shuffled into a "Hero Draft Pool" deck.
    • Players take turns drafting additional heroes from this pool until each player has their allocated number of heroes. For 2 players, Player 1 draws 2, picks one, passes the other. Player 2 then takes that hero. Repeat. For more players, each player draws 2, picks one, passes left.
  3. Unit Card Draft Rounds:
    • A separate "Unit Card Draft Pool" is created, containing all available Unit Cards.
    • Players then take turns drafting Unit Cards from this pool until each player has their allocated number of Unit Cards. Each drafted Unit Card is then added to the deck of one of their chosen heroes. Players decide which hero receives which Unit Card, allowing for synergistic deck building.
  4. Finalizing Rosters: Once all drafting rounds are complete, players reveal their full hero and unit rosters. This strategic choice of heroes and units will significantly impact the tactical options available in the upcoming scenario.

Example: In a 2-player cooperative campaign, Player A chooses Lord-Celestant Michael as their initial hero. Player B chooses Loreweaver Lyra. For the Hero Draft, the remaining 16 heroes are shuffled. Player A draws 2, picks one (e.g., Knight-Questor Kelly), and passes the other to Player B. Player B then takes that hero. This process repeats until both players have their 3 chosen heroes. Then, a separate pool of Unit Cards is prepared, and players take turns drafting 3 Unit Cards each. Player A might draft a 'Liberator Unit' card and add it to Michael's deck, and an 'Aelven Wardens' card to Kelly's deck.

Chaos vs. Order: Adversarial Game Mode

For those who seek a direct confrontation, "Abbey Dawn: Last Stand" offers an adversarial game mode where one player commands the valiant **Order Heroes** (defending), and another commands the brutal **Chaos and Destruction Heroes** (attacking).

Game Setup

  • One player selects their allocated number of Order Heroes and drafts their Unit Cards from the Order Unit Pool, adding them to their heroes' decks.
  • The other player selects their allocated number of Chaos/Destruction Heroes and drafts their Unit Cards from the Chaos/Destruction Unit Pool, adding them to their heroes' decks.
  • A specific scenario is chosen (e.g., "The First Breach" or a custom objective-based map). The scenario will define the defender's setup zone and the attacker's deployment.
  • Each hero has their own unique card deck, now supplemented by drafted Unit Cards.

Turn Structure

The game proceeds in alternating player turns, rather than distinct phases:

  1. Order Player Turn:
    • Generates Glory for their active heroes.
    • Activates each of their heroes and deployed Unit Cards one at a time, performing movement, playing cards (abilities, spells, blessings, unit deployments, quests), and resolving combat.
  2. Chaos/Destruction Player Turn:
    • Generates Glory for their active heroes.
    • Activates each of their heroes and deployed Unit Cards one at a time, performing movement, playing cards (abilities, spells, blessings, unit deployments, quests), and resolving combat.

The game continues until the scenario objectives are met or one side is entirely defeated. This mode offers a direct, competitive experience, testing tactical prowess against a thinking opponent.

Winning and Losing

The fate of Abbey Dawn hangs in the balance, determined by the heroes' success or failure. The struggle for the realms is an endless, grinding war, and sometimes, even a 'win' is merely a reprieve, a moment of respite before the next wave of darkness crashes upon the shores of hope.

  • Winning a Scenario: Players achieve victory in a scenario by successfully completing all its primary objectives before a specific turn limit is reached, or before all their valiant heroes are tragically defeated. Alternatively, players can achieve a special victory by either holding 3 active Blessing cards simultaneously on the battlefield, or by successfully casting a total of 4 Blessing cards within a single scenario.
  • Losing a Scenario: Players suffer defeat if all heroes are incapacitated or removed from play, or if a critical objective is irrevocably failed (e.g., a key NPC dies, a vital location is overrun by the enemy hordes).
  • Campaign Victory: The entire campaign is won by successfully completing all introductory scenarios and then triumphantly completing the final, climactic scenario, successfully pushing back the main invasion force and securing Abbey Dawn's future.
  • Campaign Defeat: The campaign is tragically lost if too many scenarios are lost, leading to the inevitable and devastating fall of Abbey Dawn to the forces of darkness.
